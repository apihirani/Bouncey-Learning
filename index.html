Full Table

  • 2 × 5 = 10
  • 2 × 6 = 12
  • 2 × 7 = 14
  • 2 × 8 = 16
  • 2 × 9 = 18
  • 2 × 10 = 20
💡 Memory Trick

Doubling! 2×n is always just n+n — if you can add, you can double.

⚠️ Common Mistake

Mixing up doubling with adding 2 to the previous answer instead of doubling the new number.

🌍 Real-Life Use

Pairs of shoes, pairs of socks, two wheels on a bike.

🧠 Quick Challenge

What is 2 × 11?

👩‍🏫 Teacher Tip

Have students physically pair up objects before drilling the facts numerically.

👪 Parent Tip

Ask 'how many shoes for our family?' — count people, then double.

Did You Know? 2 is the only even prime number!

🎯 Goal: Recall every 2× fact instantly by recognizing it as simple doubling.

🎯 Goal: Recall the two anchor facts (7×7, 7×8) and use them to solve any 7× fact.

💡 Memory Trick

Finger trick: hold up 10 fingers, fold down finger number n — fingers left of the fold are tens, fingers right are ones. Folding the 4th finger for 9×4 leaves 3 and 6 up — that's 36!

⚠️ Common Mistake

Folding the wrong finger, or misreading which side is tens versus ones.

🌍 Real-Life Use

Nine innings in a baseball game, nine players on a baseball team.

🧠 Quick Challenge

What is 9 × 7?

👩‍🏫 Teacher Tip

The finger trick works every time for 9×1 through 9×10 — practice it as a party trick, not just a formula.

👪 Parent Tip

Let your child teach YOU the 9s finger trick — teaching back is powerful practice.

Did You Know? Every 9× fact's digits add up to 9 — check 9×8=72: 7+2=9!

🎯 Goal: Use the finger trick to independently verify any 9× fact.
